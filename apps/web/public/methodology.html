Methodology

How ArgusOrb tracks the Starlink constellation and flags orbital anomalies.

What this page is

Every number on argusorb.io and in the weekly report traces back to a specific public TLE or an independent RF observation. This page lists the sources, the classifier rules, and the places where our current approach is provisional or wrong. If a claim on the site feels surprising, check here first.

Anomaly classifier — rule_v1

The current production classifier is a set of seven physics thresholds applied between consecutive TLEs:

  • reentry: altitude < 250 km
  • natural decay: monotonic altitude drop, B* positive, high drag
  • altitude step: Δ altitude > 10 km between consecutive TLEs
  • inclination shift: Δ inclination > 0.5°
  • eccentricity jump: e vs same-launch-batch > 2× average
  • B* sign flip: drag coefficient changes sign
  • B* magnitude jump: > 200% change without altitude change

Every flag is a candidate, not a confirmed event. The next-layer classifier (imm_ukf_v1, IMM-UKF physics-based) and eventually a physics-informed transformer (orbital_transformer_v06) will re-evaluate and can override rule_v1. We expose both verdicts in the archive so readers can compare.

What we currently can't do

  • Sub-300 km regime — IMM-UKF skips satellites below 300 km because our atmosphere model (static exponential, Vallado Table 8-4) can't resolve the 2-10× density variation driven by solar F10.7 / Ap. Rule_v1 handles these with threshold logic instead. An NRLMSISE-00 upgrade is scheduled.
  • Sub-10 cm debris — invisible in public catalog data. Optical and radar domain.
  • Real-time < 8h latency — our primary feed's cadence is 8h. Faster sources require operator-direct access we don't have.
  • Threat characterization — intent classification ("adversarial proximity", "co-planar maneuvering") is explicitly out of scope. That is threat intelligence; we are risk intelligence.

Cadence

  • TLE fetch every 8h
  • Position updates on the 3D globe every 5s
  • Anomaly analysis in-line with each TLE fetch
  • Weekly report: Sundays at 20:00 UTC (cron-generated, human-reviewed)
  • Daily backup to Cloudflare R2 at 04:00 UTC

Provenance policy

Every TLE update is stored permanently in a SQLite archive (now 256k+ records). Every classifier label is stored forever, with the classifier version that produced it. This archive is the moat: a second observer can re-run our code against our data and reach the same conclusions, or disagree visibly.

If we are wrong, it is because the data is wrong, the code is wrong, or the interpretation is wrong. All three are inspectable. This is not a promise of correctness. It is a promise of auditability.

Corrections

When an error is found after publication, the weekly-report archive keeps the original and adds a correction post. The 2026-W16 correction of the B* narrative (in the first issue) is an example. We aim to lose less trust by correcting in public than by silently editing.
